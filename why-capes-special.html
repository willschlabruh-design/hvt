Why Are These Capes Special?

Official Minecraft capes are cosmetic rewards tied to limited promotions and events — not regular in-game purchases.

Mojang releases most capes through time-limited campaigns: Twitch drops, live events, Minecraft Experience tours, and in-person conventions. When a promotion ends, new codes can no longer be earned through the official channel. That makes unused codes the only way to obtain those capes after the fact.

Limited availability

Promotional capes are offered for a defined window — often days or weeks. After that, Mojang stops issuing new codes for that campaign.

Event-exclusive releases

Some capes require attending Minecraft Experience, TwitchCon, or similar events in person. Online alternatives are not always offered.

Promotional capes

Stream drops, movie tie-ins, anniversary celebrations, and game-update quests distribute capes as marketing rewards — not through the Marketplace store.

Collector interest

Players who missed a promotion often seek unused codes later. Scarcer capes — especially in-person exclusives — tend to attract more collector demand.

Promotions that end

When Mojang closes a campaign, the only path to that cape is an unused code someone already holds — or buying an account (which violates Mojang terms and is not something we recommend).

Unused codes become rarer

Every redeemed code is consumed permanently. Over time, the pool of unredeemed codes for a finished promotion naturally shrinks.

Factual note: HVTDEVIL does not guarantee future value, resale price, or rarity rankings for any cape. Availability and Mojang policies can change. We sell unused redemption codes for official promotional capes.
